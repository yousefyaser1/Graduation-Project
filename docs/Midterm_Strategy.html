3. Expected Questions + Safe Answers

Question Safe Answer
"What's your current accuracy?" Around 90% on our validation set of 325 images across 3 classes. We're actively working on improving this, particularly for Eczema which is our most challenging class.
"Why not use a cloud-based model?" Privacy is critical for medical images. Our offline approach ensures patient data never leaves the device. This also makes the app usable in areas with poor connectivity.
"How does the VAE work exactly?" It's trained only on normal skin images. It learns what 'normal' looks like, then flags anything with high reconstruction error as potentially abnormal. We use MSE with a threshold of 0.15.
"Why these 3 diseases?" Tinea, Eczema, and Acne are the most common dermatological conditions that are visually diagnosable. We consulted with Dr. Yasser Shaban, a dermatologist, to validate this scope.
"What about other skin diseases?" Expanding the disease set is in our future work. The architecture supports it — we'd retrain the CNN with additional classes. For scope, we focused on 3 high-prevalence conditions.
"How big is the model on-device?" We're targeting under 250MB total including all models and local database. TFLite quantization helps reduce this significantly.
"Is Score-CAM running on mobile?" We have the algorithm implemented and generating correct heatmaps. We're in the process of optimizing it for mobile inference speed — Score-CAM is computationally heavier than classification alone.
"Why EfficientNet over MobileNetV2?" MobileNetV2 reached about 84% in our experiments. EfficientNet's compound scaling gave us a significant jump. We're evaluating the tradeoff between accuracy and model size for the final deployment.
"What's your dataset size?" We curated from DermNet and Dermatology Atlas — thousands of images across the 3 classes plus normal skin. We applied augmentation including flips, rotations, brightness shifts, and zoom for the CNN training set.
"How do you handle misclassification?" The confidence score is shown to the user with a clear disclaimer that this is a screening tool, not a diagnosis. We also provide the XAI heatmap so clinicians can verify what the model focused on.

If pressed on accuracy or technical depth:

"We have a systematic improvement plan. We've been iterating through architectures and training strategies, and we have concrete next steps to push accuracy further. We'd rather present honest current results than overstate where we are."
This answer is truthful, professional, and earns respect.
